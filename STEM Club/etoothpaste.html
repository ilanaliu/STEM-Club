The Exciting, Messy and Fun Elephant Toothpaste!

Watch a demo

What is elephant toothpaste?

Ever seen toothpaste fit for an elephant? Well, this foamy, colorful science experiment looks just like that—a huge explosion of bubbles that oozes out of a bottle like magic! Elephant toothpaste is a popular science activity that creates a big, bubbly reaction using just a few simple ingredients: hydrogen peroxide (which breaks down into water and oxygen), yeast or potassium iodide (which acts as a helper called a catalyst), warm water, and dish soap. When these mix together, they cause a super fast reaction that blows up the soap into a foamy fountain!

Here's how it works:

The hydrogen peroxide breaks down into oxygen gas and water. But the reaction happens so fast (thanks to the catalyst) that the oxygen gets trapped in the dish soap, creating tons of bubbles all at once! The result? A super cool, safe chemical reaction that looks like a giant tube of toothpaste squeezed out in one big blast—just the right size for an elephant! You can even add food coloring to make it rainbow-colored, or shape the foam with your hands (once it cools down). It’s messy, it's foamy, and it's an awesome way to learn about chemical reactions, catalysts, and gases.

What You’ll Need (Kid-Safe Version):

  • 1 clean, empty plastic bottle (16 oz works great!)
  • 1 packet of dry yeast (about 1 tablespoon)
  • ½ cup warm water (not hot!)
  • ½ cup 3% hydrogen peroxide (you can buy this at a pharmacy)
  • A few squirts of dish soap
  • A few drops of food coloring (optional, but fun!)
  • A small bowl for mixing
  • Safety goggles & a tray or pan to catch the foam

Step-by-Step Instructions:

  • 1. Set up your space
  • 2. Put on your safety goggles to protect your eyes.
  • 3. Place the bottle on a tray or in a pan—it’s going to get messy (in a good way)!
  • 4. Add the fun stuff to the bottle.
  • 5. Pour the ½ cup of hydrogen peroxide into the bottle.
  • 6. Add a few drops of food coloring if you want colorful foam.
  • 7. Squeeze in a big squirt of dish soap and gently swirl the bottle to mix.
  • 8. Make your yeast mix.
  • 9. In a separate bowl, mix the dry yeast with the warm water. Stir for about 30 seconds until it’s kind of soupy.
  • 10. Time for the magic!
  • 11. Quickly pour the yeast mixture into the bottle and step back...
  • 12. Watch it explode!
  • 13. A huge stream of bubbly foam will burst out of the bottle like a foamy volcano. It might even wiggle like a snake!
